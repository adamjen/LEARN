# Six Seconds Model of Emotional Intelligence

## Overview

The Six Seconds Model is a practical, action-oriented framework for developing emotional intelligence. Developed by the Six Seconds Network, this model focuses on making EQ accessible and applicable in everyday life. The model is built around three core pursuits and eight specific competencies.

## The Three Pursuits

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              SIX SECONDS EQ MODEL                        │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  KNOW YOURSELF    │  CHOOSE YOURSELF    │  GIVE YOURSELF │
│  (Self-Awareness) │  (Self-Management)  │  (Purpose)     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

The model organizes EQ into three sequential but interconnected pursuits:

### 1. Know Yourself - Grow Self-Awareness

Understanding your emotions, patterns, and motivations.

### 2. Choose Yourself - Do What You Mean to Do

Intentional action based on self-awareness and values.

### 3. Give Yourself - Do It for a Reason

Living with purpose and contributing to something larger.

## The Eight Competencies

### Pursuit 1: Know Yourself

#### Competency 1: Self-Awareness

**Purpose**: See clearly what's happening in the moment.

**Key Skills:**

- Recognize your emotions as they occur
- Notice your thoughts and patterns
- Understand your triggers and reactions
- Identify your strengths and limitations

**Practical Application:**

- Pause and notice: "What am I feeling right now?"
- Journal about emotional experiences
- Ask: "What triggered this reaction?"
- Practice mindfulness to observe without judgment

#### Competency 2: Self-Regulation

**Purpose**: Choose how to express your emotions.

**Key Skills:**

- Manage emotional impulses
- Adapt to changing circumstances
- Maintain integrity under pressure
- Handle stress effectively

**Practical Application:**

- Take a breath before reacting
- Consider multiple responses to situations
- Practice stress management techniques
- Develop healthy coping mechanisms

#### Competency 3: Self-Motivation

**Purpose**: Engage your heart and mind toward goals.

**Key Skills:**

- Connect with your core motivations
- Pursue goals with energy and persistence
- Maintain optimism despite setbacks
- Find intrinsic rewards in your work

**Practical Application:**

- Identify what truly motivates you
- Set meaningful, value-aligned goals
- Celebrate progress and small wins
- Reframe challenges as opportunities

### Pursuit 2: Choose Yourself

#### Competency 4: Empathy

**Purpose**: Sense what others need and feel.

**Key Skills:**

- Recognize emotions in others
- Understand different perspectives
- Listen with genuine interest
- Respond appropriately to others' needs

**Practical Application:**

- Practice active listening
- Ask clarifying questions
- Notice non-verbal cues
- Validate others' feelings

#### Competency 5: Decision Making

**Purpose**: Make balanced, values-aligned choices.

**Key Skills:**

- Consider consequences of choices
- Balance needs of self, others, and situation
- Apply emotional data to decisions
- Make choices aligned with values

**Practical Application:**

- Pause to consider options
- Evaluate impact on all stakeholders
- Check alignment with personal values
- Learn from past decisions

#### Competency 6: Purpose

**Purpose**: Know what you want and move toward it.

**Key Skills:**

- Identify your vision and goals
- Align daily actions with larger purpose
- Pursue meaningful objectives
- Stay focused on what matters

**Practical Application:**

- Define your personal vision
- Set goals that excite and motivate
- Break goals into actionable steps
- Regularly review and adjust goals

### Pursuit 3: Give Yourself

#### Competency 7: Nurturing Relationships

**Purpose**: Build and maintain meaningful connections.

**Key Skills:**

- Create rapport and trust
- Communicate effectively
- Resolve conflicts constructively
- Maintain connections over time

**Practical Application:**

- Invest time in relationships
- Practice appreciation and gratitude
- Handle disagreements respectfully
- Follow through on commitments

#### Competency 8: Contributing to Society

**Purpose**: Make a positive difference in the world.

**Key Skills:**

- Identify ways to serve others
- Take action toward meaningful causes
- Collaborate for common good
- Leave things better than you found them

**Practical Application:**

- Identify causes you care about
- Find ways to contribute regularly
- Volunteer or support meaningful initiatives
- Look for opportunities to help

## The Three Pursuits in Detail

### Know Yourself: Grow Self-Awareness

**Why It Matters:**
You can't manage what you don't understand. Self-awareness is the foundation of emotional intelligence.

**Key Questions:**

- What am I experiencing right now?
- Why am I feeling this way?
- What patterns do I notice?
- What are my strengths and limitations?

**Development Practices:**

- Daily emotion check-ins
- Mindfulness meditation
- Journaling about experiences
- Seeking feedback from others
- Reflecting on emotional triggers

### Choose Yourself: Do What You Mean to Do

**Why It Matters:**
Self-awareness without action is incomplete. This pursuit is about intentional, values-aligned behavior.

**Key Questions:**

- What choice do I want to make?
- Does this align with my values?
- How will I express my emotions?
- What action serves my purpose?

**Development Practices:**

- Pause before reacting
- Consider multiple options
- Evaluate consequences
- Make conscious choices
- Follow through consistently

### Give Yourself: Do It for a Reason

**Why It Matters:**
True fulfillment comes from contributing to something beyond yourself. This pursuit connects personal growth with meaningful contribution.

**Key Questions:**

- What do I want to contribute?
- How can I serve others?
- What's my larger purpose?
- How do I create positive impact?

**Development Practices:**

- Identify meaningful causes
- Find ways to contribute regularly
- Build relationships purposefully
- Collaborate for common good
- Reflect on your impact

## The Six Seconds Process

The model provides a simple three-step process for any situation:

### Step 1: Pause

- Stop and notice what's happening
- Create space between stimulus and response
- Ask: "What am I experiencing?"

### Step 2: Choose

- Consider your options
- Align with your values and purpose
- Ask: "What choice serves my purpose?"

### Step 3: Act

- Take intentional action
- Follow through consistently
- Reflect on the outcome
- Ask: "How did this serve my purpose?"

## Practical Application Examples

### In Conflict Situations

**Using Six Seconds Model:**

1. **Pause**: Notice your emotions in the conflict
2. **Choose**: Decide on a constructive response
3. **Act**: Communicate your needs calmly
4. **Reflect**: Learn from the experience

### In Decision Making

**Using Six Seconds Model:**

1. **Pause**: Notice your emotional state
2. **Choose**: Consider options aligned with values
3. **Act**: Make the decision and follow through
4. **Reflect**: Evaluate the outcome

### In Relationships

**Using Six Seconds Model:**

1. **Pause**: Notice your and others' emotions
2. **Choose**: Respond with empathy and care
3. **Act**: Build connection through action
4. **Reflect**: Strengthen the relationship

## Six Seconds and the Tone Scale

### Correlation Between EQ and Tone

| EQ Level    | Corresponding Tone Range |
| ----------- | ------------------------ |
| High EQ     | +3.5 to +4.5+            |
| Moderate EQ | +2.5 to +3.4             |
| Low EQ      | Below +2.5               |

### How EQ Helps Maintain Higher Tones

- **Self-Awareness**: Recognize tone shifts early
- **Self-Regulation**: Choose responses that elevate tone
- **Self-Motivation**: Maintain positive emotional states
- **Empathy**: Build connections that support higher tones
- **Purpose**: Find meaning that sustains positive emotions
