## Workplace Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Resistant Team Member

**ID:** `workplace-001`  
**Difficulty:** 1 (Beginner)  
**Current Tone:** +1.5 (Anger)

#### Scenario Description

Alex has been missing deadlines and seems disengaged in team meetings. You need to address this without damaging the relationship. Alex used to be a high-performing team member.

#### Current State

- **Tone Level:** +1.5 (Anger)
- **ARC State:** Appreciation: 5/10, Reality: 5/10, Communication: 5/10

#### Response Options

| Option | Text                                                        | Tone Impact | ARC Impact          | EQ Component    | Explanation                                                                                                                | Why This Response                                                                                               |
| ------ | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ----------- | ------------------- | --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| A      | "Tell Alex directly that their performance is unacceptable" | -2.0        | A: -3, R: -1, C: -2 | Social Skills   | Direct confrontation at low tone escalates conflict. Alex is already at Anger (+1.5), so this will likely push them lower. | At Anger tone, direct confrontation triggers defensiveness. The person needs empathy first before any feedback. |
| B      | "Ask Alex privately if everything is okay"                  | +2.0        | A: +2, R: +1, C: +2 | Empathy         | Showing care and opening communication builds Appreciation and Reality while maintaining good Communication.               | Empathy builds Appreciation by showing you care. This creates safety for honest dialogue about Reality.         |
| C      | "Share your perspective on their performance"               | 0.0         | A: -1, R: +1, C: +1 | Self-Regulation | Sharing perspective can help Reality but may reduce Appreciation if not done carefully.                                    | Self-regulation helps you stay calm, but without Appreciation first, the message won't land well.               |
| D      | "Ignore it and hope it resolves itself"                     | -1.0        | A: -2, R: -2, C: -3 | None            | Avoidance doesn't solve the problem and damages all ARC elements.                                                          | Avoidance signals you don't care (low Appreciation) and avoids Reality, making Communication impossible.        |

#### Correct Answer

**Option B** - This builds Appreciation through showing care, improves Reality by creating space for honest dialogue, and enhances Communication through open-ended questions.

#### Learning Points

- At low tones, empathy and appreciation matter more than direct confrontation
- Communication quality depends on Appreciation and Reality being present
- EQ component: Empathy helps raise tone by showing you care about the person
- ARC principle: Strengthening Appreciation first makes Reality and Communication more effective

- **ARC State:** Appreciation: 3/10, Reality: 4/10, Communication: 6/10

#### Response Options

| Option | Text                                                                     | Tone Impact | ARC Impact          | EQ Component    | Explanation                                                                                                                        | Why This Response                                                                                                           |
| ------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------- | ------------------- | --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |

| B      | "Immediately explain why the delay wasn't your fault"                    | -1.0        | A: -2, R: -1, C: +1 | Self-Awareness  | Defending yourself increases their antagonism and damages Appreciation.                                                            | Defensiveness at Blame tone triggers more blame. ## Scoring Logic Reference

### Tone Impact Guidelines

| Impact Range   | Meaning                                        |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| +3.0 or higher | Excellent response - significantly raises tone |
| +1.0 to +2.9   | Good response - moderately raises tone         |
| 0.0            | Neutral - no change in tone                    |
| -1.0 to -2.9   | Poor response - lowers tone                    |
| -3.0 or lower  | Very poor - significantly lowers tone          |

### ARC Impact Guidelines

| Component     | Positive Impact | Negative Impact |
| ------------- | --------------- | --------------- |
| Appreciation  | +2 to +3        | -2 to -3        |
| Reality       | +2 to +3        | -1 to -3        |
| Communication | +2 to +3        | -1 to -3        |

### EQ Component Mapping

| EQ Component    | When to Apply                              |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Self-Awareness  | When person needs to recognize their state |
| Self-Regulation | When person needs to manage their emotions |
| Motivation      | When person needs encouragement            |
| Empathy         | When person needs to feel understood       |
| Social Skills   | When group dynamics are involved           |
